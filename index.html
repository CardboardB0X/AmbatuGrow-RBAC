1. What is an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) System?

Think of an ERP as the "central brain" of a company. In a standard business, different departments (like warehousing, purchasing, and customer support) often keep their own separate notes. This creates "data silos" where one team doesn't know what the other is doing.

An ERP eliminates this by feeding all data into one shared database. When a transaction happens, everyone sees the update instantly:

  • Real-Time Sync: The moment a customer purchases an item on the webstore, the inventory counts drop automatically, and logistics immediately schedules shipment. No manual phone calls or emails required.

2. Why System Boundaries Matter

To prevent fraud, theft, and critical operational mistakes, the ERP system establishes strict boundaries between different jobs (separation of duties):

  • Inventory vs. Purchasing: The warehouse operators who handle physical boxes can log stock levels, but they are blocked from approving purchase budgets. Only Procurement Specialists can sign off on spending.
  • Dual-Authorization Gate: A general employee can create a "shopping cart" request (Purchase Requisition), but it is locked from being sent to suppliers until a Department Manager clicks "Approve."

3. What is Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)?

RBAC is a security system that works like **job-based digital keycards**. Instead of assigning individual keys to every employee, keys are linked directly to job roles (like *WMS Operator* or *Customer Support Rep*).

When a user logs in, the system reads their assigned role ID and grants them only the specific clearances required to perform their daily duties, blocking all other modules.

Procurement & Supplier Inbound Lifecycle (How We Stock Up)

This flow handles ordering and receiving supplies from external vendors:

  • Step 1: Requesting (Requisition) - An employee submits a list of needed supplies. The request starts as 'Pending' status.
  • Step 2: Sign-Off (Approval) - The department manager checks budgets and approves the request, moving it to 'Approved' status.
  • Step 3: Creating and Dispatching the Purchase Order (PO) - Once a request is approved, a formal PO contract must be generated and dispatched:
    • Manual PO Creation: A Procurement Specialist reviews the approved requisition, queries the Product_Suppliers catalog to identify the preferred vendor, extracts contract prices, and manually compiles the PO.
    • Automated PO Creation: If WMS stock levels drop below safety thresholds, the system automatically pulls the SKU's preferred vendor and pricing from the database, bypassing manual entry to instantly generate a PO.
    • Issuance & Tracking: The PO is sent to the vendor portal, shifting status from Sent to Confirmed once accepted.
  • Step 4: Unloading (Goods Receipt) - The vendor delivers boxes to the dock. Warehouse operators verify counts, log a 'Stock-In' transaction, and WMS increments inventory balances.

E-Commerce Order Sync & Outbound Fulfillment (How We Ship to Customers)

This flow covers processing customer orders and shipping them out:

  • Step 1: Sync Checkout - Customer completes checkout on the online store. The integration instantly imports customer details and creates a Sales Order in the database.
  • Step 2: Stock Check - The database automatically queries WMS inventory locations to confirm items are available.
  • Step 3: Picking & Packing (Stock-Out) - Warehouse operators retrieve the products from assigned storage bins, package them, and write a 'Stock-Out' transaction log.
  • Step 4: Dispatch (Logistics) - The carrier picks up the shipment, updates transit logs, and marks the status as 'Delivered' upon customer delivery receipt.

Supply Chain & Logistics Management (SCM) (How We Route and Track Shipments)

Supply Chain Management (SCM) coordinates the movement of physical goods across the entire business lifecycle:

  • Inbound Logistics: Tracks materials coming from suppliers. Once a Purchase Order (PO) is approved, a Shipment record is opened to track transit updates as freight travels to our loading docks.
  • Outbound Logistics: Manages delivery carriers after picking. SCM links destination addresses to carrier systems, logs tracking numbers, and monitors transit status until final delivery is confirmed at the customer's doorstep.

Database Architecture Overview

AmbatuGrow uses a highly normalized relational database layout to guarantee transactional integrity (ACID compliance) and prevent data duplication. Below is the updated data dictionary reflecting the official tables and RBAC entities.

Table Name Domain Layer Primary Key Foreign Keys & Constraints Operational Description
Roles Core Master Data role_id - Stores security clearance names and definitions.
Users Core Master Data user_id role_id Stores credential logins and link back to their Role.
Permissions RBAC (Security) permission_id - Lists all valid atomic actions across all modules.
Role_Permissions RBAC (Security) role_id, permission_id role_id, permission_id Junction table mapping actions to specific clearance roles.
Addresses Core Master Data address_id - Centralized addresses for warehouses, suppliers, customers.
Products PIM / WMS product_id category_id, uom_id, currency_id Stores active SKU specifications and pricing metadata.
Inventory_Locations PIM / WMS inventory_id product_id, warehouse_id, zone_id Quantifies items physically located in warehouse zones.
Product_Suppliers Procurement product_id, supplier_id product_id, supplier_id Junction catalog mapping items to suppliers with custom costs.
Purchase_Orders Procurement po_id supplier_id, payment_term_id, currency_id, created_by Outbound purchasing agreements sent to external vendors.
Sales_Orders E-Commerce order_id customer_id, payment_term_id, currency_id Inbound client purchase agreements imported from E-Commerce.
Tickets Helpdesk ticket_id customer_id, order_id Incident tickets for post-sale support or inquiries.
Shipments Logistics shipment_id destination_address_id Tracks in-transit or outbound logistics shipments.
